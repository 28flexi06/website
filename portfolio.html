Selected projects

Genomics
Comparative Genomics of NHEJ Accessory Proteins Across Mycobacterium Species
How does genome size shape DNA repair machinery? We built an end-to-end bioinformatics pipeline to profile five NHEJ repair proteins across 20 Mycobacterium species — combining genome annotation (BAKTA), protein homology search (BLASTp), multiple sequence alignment (MAFFT), and phylogenetic tree construction (IQ-TREE, OrthoFinder). Statistical analysis in R revealed that UvrD copy number scales significantly with genome size (r = 0.52, p = 0.018), and that smaller genomes may exhibit lower NHEJ fidelity — with downstream implications for drug resistance in pathogens like M. tuberculosis.

Can You Beat a Brain-Inspired Neural Network?
Most AI learns from labeled data. Ours didn't. We engineered an unsupervised neural network using Oja's rule — a biologically-inspired learning algorithm modeled after how real neurons strengthen connections — that discovers visual patterns entirely on its own. The result: 98.3% accuracy on MNIST and 50.7% on CIFAR-10, with zero manual labeling.
